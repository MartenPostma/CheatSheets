SVM

Information

Support Vector Machines, or SVM, are typically used for classification purposes.

The goal is to predict hyperplanes that divide the training examples into separate classes.

A support vector is a data point that is closest to a hyperplane.

The further away a data point is to a hyperplane, the more confident we are about the prediction.

The distance between a hyperplane and a support vector is called the margin.

In training, the goal is to maximize the margin.

If the patterns in the data are non-linear, we have to move to higher dimensions in order to find hyperplanes, which is computationally expensive.

This is where the kernel trick comes in, which allows us to remain in the original feature space.
